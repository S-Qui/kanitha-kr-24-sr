About Christian Education

In the Strategic Planning process of the PCEA Head Office which is in the process of being rolled out, among the many areas of opportunity and growth the following directions have been identified: –
Strategic Objective: To transform the Church, Families, Relationships, and Society through TEACHING Christ-Centred Knowledge.
About nine Strategic Initiatives have been identified among them: –

  1. Entrench and Expand PPI (Program for Pastoral Instruction) in learning Institutions. There are materials available from Head Office (prepared by Scripture Union in conjunction with the Churches amongst them PCEA) for Class one to Eight. These will be aligned with time to comply with the CBC model. Pastoral materials for high school are in the process of being developed.
  2. Establish and Expand Chaplaincy service in Institutions – Learning Institutions, Hospitals, Financial Institutions, Industries, Airports/Aviation, Transport (Motorbikes, Tuktuks, matatus, buses, lorries etc), etc.
  3. Design and Implement Comprehensive Family Programs – Courtship, Pre-marital counseling, Parenting, Domestic Workers program, Widows/Widowers, Orphans, Singles, Senior Citizens etc.
  4. Establish and Initiate Christian Professional Fellowships – Teachers, Lawyers, Accountants, etc.

Practice and Procedure Mandate

The Constitutional mandate for Christian Education is in Chapter 27 – Acts Constituting Standing Committees and particularly ACT 19 (Pages 305-312). In a summary…

  • 19.4: Education is a vital asset in which to invest for the building of the Nation.
  • 19.5: The Church is concerned about Education in its life and work. The Church promotes education for the total well-being of the Nation and its people.
  • 19.109: CE pays special attention to the teaching of Christian Religious Education in schools as well as drawing the attention of those concerned with the pastoral needs of our education institutions.
  • 19.111: CE shall be a link with the outside bodies that work and demonstrate support as far as possible for service to the people.
  • 19.112: CE shall seek to educate, inform, and alert the Church about educational development, develop and maintain active interest for the welfare of educational institutions.
  • 19.113: Christian Education understands as its responsibility the relevance of the Christian faith and tradition to social, political, and economic issues and enhance of moral Christian values among the people by every means possible.
We could also add another mandate from The Great Commission (Mathew 28:19-20) where the last instruction is to TEACH, and the Great Commandment (Luke 10:27) where LOVE is the DOING part of our Christian Faith.
